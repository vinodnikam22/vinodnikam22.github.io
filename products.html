Load washer

Customized Load washers
Load washers are used for Determination of the torque-tension relationship for a threaded fastener allowing the appropriate tightening torque to be determined. Such tests will allow the nut factor (sometimes referred to as the torque coefficient or k factor) to be determined. and the overall coefficient of friction. By completing several similar tests, the variation in the torque-tension relationship, due to friction variation, can be established for an application. Reactive Torque Sensor

Reactive Torque Sensor
A reaction transducer has two mounting flanges. One face is fixed to ground or a rigid structural member and the other to the rotating or rotary element. Rotation generates shear forces between the flanges. S. Himmelstein and Company measures these forces with precision strain gages. These incorporate wire that responds to being stretched or compressed with strand small changes in electrical resistance. While the change is subtle, it can be detected effectively in a reaction torque sensor by incorporating four gages into a Wheatstone Bridge arrangement.
